Classic Fluffy Buttermilk Pancakes

⏱ Prep Time: 15 mins 🔥 Difficulty: Easy

Description: Skip the boxed mix. This simple, scratch-made recipe yields the thickest, fluffiest pancakes you'll ever make. The secret is not overmixing the batter!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups All-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp Granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp Baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1 1/4 cups Buttermilk (or regular whole milk)
  • 1 large Egg
  • 3 tbsp Unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 1 tsp Pure vanilla extract
  • Butter or cooking spray for the pan
  • Warm maple syrup and fresh berries (for serving)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt until well combined.
  2. Mix the wet ingredients: In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Combine wet and dry: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Use a spatula to gently fold them together until just combined. Do not overmix! A few lumps in the batter are completely fine and will make the pancakes fluffier.
  4. Rest the batter: Let the batter sit for 5 minutes while you heat up your pan.
  5. Heat the pan: Heat a large non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly coat it with butter or cooking spray.
  6. Cook: Pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook for 2-3 minutes until bubbles begin to form on the surface and the edges look set.
  7. Flip: Carefully flip the pancakes with a wide spatula and cook for another 1-2 minutes until the bottom is golden brown.
  8. Serve: Serve immediately in a warm stack, topped with a pat of butter, fresh berries, and a generous pour of maple syrup.
